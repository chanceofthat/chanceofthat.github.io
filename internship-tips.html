Maximize Your Summer Internship

5 Ways to Make the Most of Your Summer Internship
So congratulations, you just landed your dream internship. But how do you ensure that your time is well spent? Here are five tips to maximize your internship potential. 
During your internship, it is important to start work early if possible. Depending on the company you intern for, you might not have scheduled hours. However, this doesn't mean that you should sleep in and show up around noon. By arriving early in the morning, perhaps even before your colleagues, it shows that you are passionate about your work. Another benefit of showing up early, is that it gives you the opportunity to have a longer work day, giving yourself more time if you find yourself up against a deadline.
If your internship is in a field that you envision yourself having a career in, you should attempt to meet as many of the other employees as you can. By networking with your colleagues, you can gain valuable connections for when you are searching for a full time position. Apart from the connections, you also have the opportunity to learn some amazing stuff from experts in the field.
​Depending on your internship, you may have a presentation to show your manager what you've accomplished during your summer. It is important that you take the time to prepare for your presentation, to the point where you can present your project or research naturally, and without notes. Even a great idea can get lost with a lack-luster or unrehearsed presentation; so take the time to practice more than you think you should, to ensure that you can convey your accomplishments with ease. 
During the summer, you may find yourself with poorer sleep habits than during the school year, but in order to perform your best at your internship, it is important to get a good night's rest. Not only will you find it easier to get to work earlier, the benefits of which I mentioned before, but you will also make less mistakes during the day and find ideas more readily available. While it is acceptable to relax a bit during the summer, be sure to keep in mind that your performance at your internship should be a top priority.
Regardless of which company you have the opportunity to intern for, you will most likely be surrounded by experts in your field of study. While information learned in a classroom or textbook can be dated, your colleagues will be using cutting-edge practices. This out-of-classroom knowledge and experience is what will set you apart when looking for a full time position, making it vital to absorb as much as you can. Maximize this opportunity by asking your coworkers questions when you can, and exploring the way your company does things.
Hopefully these tips can set you on the path to a successful internship, and with any luck, a return offer!
